Challenge Summary

If you’re planning an outdoor event—like a vacation, a hike on a trail, or fishing on a lake—it would be good to know the chances of adverse weather for the time and location you are considering. There are many types of Earth observation data that can provide information on weather conditions for a particular location and day of the year. Your challenge is to construct an app with a personalized interface that enables users to conduct a customized query to tell them the likelihood of “very hot,” “very cold,” “very windy,” “very wet,” or “very uncomfortable” conditions for the location and time they specify. More info can be found at the official NASA Space Apps website.

All text above is credited and copied from the official NASA Space Apps website.


Our Task

Our task is to essentially create an app that allows people to see how their weather conditions are going to be at a given time in a given location. What we used to accomplish this is Unity for the actual data reader, Python for the back-end data interpretation and usage, and Website development languages (HTML, CSS, JS) to make the interface to use the app and get more details, like the details you are getting right now.


Why this Task?

Weather impacts everyone on a daily basis, and having a tool that provides accurate updates about weather in any situation can be extremely valuable. This made the idea both relevant and interesting to us. We chose this task because we felt there was a lot of potential for a weather tracking app, and we felt that our specialized skills of website development, Python, and Unity fit this task well. In our opinions, our skills lie at about the Intermediate level in terms of coding and this matched that level.
